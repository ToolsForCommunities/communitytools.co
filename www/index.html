Let's talk about communities.

Working from a co-working space or a big open office is a blessing. Or it should be. At least, that was the idea. But at the end of the day, it's a place filled with strangers and opportunities that never arrive.

When you arrive as a newcomer you feel that it's impossible to meet anyone, and as a community builder, you feel that you're always missing the connections that have to be made.

We know this kind of problems quite well. That's why we teamed up with Google for Startups to start developing tools to improve the experience in digital and physical communities.

After a comprehensive research, we launched our first tool: the directory, a website where you can meet everyone in your community. You can see who works where, their skillset, and if they need help with something. You can add your profile and your startup, if you have one.

You can see one of our directories by clicking here.

Anyone with an admin account can review and edit the info of every community member from a simple dashboard. It's pretty easy. We like easy.

In the last couple of months, we've been helping communities in more than 60 countries at co-working spaces like Google for Startups, Correos Labs and Gaza Sky Geeks, and accelerators like Techstars and Tetuan Valley.

If you want your own directory, discuss ideas for a new tool or just to say hi, feel free to use our chatbot or drop us a line to hi@communitytools.co

We speak English and Spanish fluently, and we're learning Portuguese, Italian, German and Arabic (because we love languages!)

We'll love to hear from you,
Carlos and Ivo, co-founders of Community Tools and Commit Sans.
